HCI Labweeks

Day 1: The Startup

On the first day, we got introduced to the Lab weeks and got to know more about what we will do. Then we split up into our assigned groups and got to know each other and the specific topic that we were working with.

Wearables!

That’s the topic I did get with my four groupmates. What do we do now? Is what we thought. We were supposed to work based on the scrum method. So, we’ve made a scrum board and asked ourselves what we need to do for day one. We decided to do some brainstorming and mind mapping to come up with an idea to make. We talked about the topic wearables, so what reasons are there, what wearables exist, where can they be used for and on what areas. We talked about our hobbies, experiences with wearables and problems we have on the daily life what we maybe can use.

We researched what elements we needed if we wanted the idea to come alive. We found out that we needed, for a basic first prototype, an Arduino board, and some sort of Vibration sensor. We got those from our lecturers and started to solder one of the vibration sensors to cables of the Arduino board, so we could later on connect them with the board and therefore the code. After the soldering, we connected it to the board and with the help of some YouTube videos, Alec started coding. But even after trying multiple times, it still wouldn’t work, and we didn’t know why, so we decided to try it with another sensor, which wasn’t soldered and connected it through some jumper cables. To our excitement, this worked and we were very surprised by the strength of the vibration. Since we only had three sensors, one of which with only one cable, we decided to try the soldered one again, by removing it from the Arduino cables. Unfortunately, it still didn’t work, which for us meant that we had to order some ourselves online, to get them the following day. By the end of the day, we had ordered the sensors from TinyTronics, and sat together for a small discussion on what our next steps would be. We decided that we still had to buy some slippers, test all the motors/sensors and get the motors to be controlled by code (change its behaviour). We also needed to ask ourselves the question of how we wanted the product design to be. We ended the meeting with one of the lecturers summing up the day and telling us what we can expect from tomorrow.

We chose the subjects music and festivals, because we all have a connection with this and is fun for everyone. After going deeper on the both topics, we thought about different inputs and outputs and what people can do with it. Finally, we came up with the idea to track the rhythm of a song someone plays on their phone/laptop or another device with Wi-Fi and give impulses by vibrations in someone’s shoes e.g. insoles. In that case people can wear this in festivals, concerts, parties but also at home. Users can feel the rhythm and it gives them an extra dimension.

To make the vibration work, we had to solder a larger cable as an extension for testing and for connecting it to the Arduino board. The vibration motor can have a maximum of 3 volt and the cables had to be plugged into the ground and one in 3,5. Instead of using digitalWrite in the code we used analogWrite, so that we didn’t had to use the Arduino board but just doing it analogue. The first motor we had just didn’t work what took us some time. We think it was broken because of the soldering. Another one worked with jumper cables. The code we’ve used didn’t influence the vibration. For testing and our end product we need more vibration motors so people can feel it in their whole foot/feet, which we ordered at tiny tronics.

The slipper you can see in the final logo had three different colours, and the colours are shown on the places where the vibration motors can be found.


During working on the vibration motors (three in one) we found out there was not enough volt what the Arduino board can handle. The Arduino board can handle five volts in total, but each motor needs three. So, we bought a battery with nine volt. The problem is solved, and it worked.

Later on we heard that the vibration motors works as a kind of dynamo and leads energy, even when we put them off they turn further. That’s why the stream of energy goes against the direction of the normal circuit and is harmful for the Arduino Board. With a diode the stream will just go one way, in that way it will only go to the ground and not back into the Arduino board. We needed also a transistor which can be used as a sort of switch and it works on volts. If a high voltage comes in, it will go open and when the voltage goes down it will close. Another new material we will use is a capacitor that works like a buffer for the energy.

Our product: Fliphop

Our concept revolves around the feel-good festival experience. In order for you to share those amazing memories with your friends, the slippers will help you to never lose them completely. The idea is to bring people together with a proximity device that vibrates stronger or softer depending on the distance between you and your friend. The LED lights and vibration motors will tell you how far away you are from your friends.

The flip flops will be equipped with LED lights, vibrating motors and a location based system. The two available settings are “find my friends” or simply “off”. The location based system will help you to find out if and how close you are to your friend, by indicating the proximity through LED lights and the vibrating sensors. The vibrating motors will help to determine the location without having to look down when you can’t while the LED lights will be more precise and easy to read. The product can be used in several ways. Not only can it help you find the general direction of your friends when you lose them, it can also aid in pinpointing them when you know their approximate location.
